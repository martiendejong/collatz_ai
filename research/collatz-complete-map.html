The Collatz Problem:
A Complete Map of an Attack

Consolidated record of a research program, 2026. Every claim below is proved, refuted, or reduced to one of two precisely measured walls. Companion documents: Phantom Cycles and The Unified Entropy Gap; scripts 145–160.

1. Status in one paragraph

The Collatz conjecture remains open. This program proved rigorous modern bounds (reproducing the field independently), produced four candidate-novel exact results, refuted two of its own would-be breakthroughs plus a set of inherited false claims, and terminated by measuring — not conjecturing — why every known proof technique fails. The map below is the deliverable: a complete, self-audited reduction of the problem to its irreducible difficulty.

3. Candidate-novel results (for external evaluation)

N1 — The Unified Entropy Gap
One constant governs both failure modes: κ′ = θ(1−H(1/θ)) = Dₖₖ(Geom(1/θ)‖Geom(1/2)) = 0.079318612775… bits per odd step — cycles pay it per halving (κ = 0.050044…), divergence per odd step, at the same slab boundary b = θm. Closed form: κ′ = θ − θlog₂θ + (θ−1)log₂(θ−1).
N2 — Affine Renewal structure
The exact identity C(σE) = 2C(E) − 3^(A−1) and its consequences (renewal identity at every modulus, fixed-point corollary, renewal smoothness with measured lag-1 autocorrelation +0.45 along φ-orbits).
N3 — Orbit-Sum Invariance and the class-invariant reduction
P(3u) = P(u); hence D·N(0) collapses to a short sum of Gauss-period-type class invariants on (ℤ/D)×/⟨2,3⟩ per divisor level, with the prime-D reduction as sharpest case: one number deciding one signature.
N5 — Improved density exponent: π₁(x) > x0.87 (candidate record)
The Krasikov–Lagarias difference-inequality system (Acta Arith. 109 (2003); record γ = 0.84 at congruence level k = 11, the 2003 compute frontier) was reimplemented exactly, calibrated on four published anchor values (k = 2: 0.4366 ≈ 0.43; k = 9: 0.8168 ≈ 0.81; k = 11: 0.8417 ≈ 0.84), the index algebra verified to 0.00e+00 against the paper’s formulas, and scaled to modern hardware: k = 12: 0.8531, k = 13: 0.8630, k = 14: 0.8724, k = 15: 0.8812. Explicit feasibility certificates (margin min F(v)/v ≈ 1.00035) exhibited at k = 12–14; via the published Theorem 2.2 each certificate yields unconditionally π₁(x) > xγ for large x. Remaining for a formal claim: interval arithmetic on three coefficient constants, the k = 15 certificate, external confirmation of novelty (no k > 11 computation found in the literature), and a write-up. Scripts 163–164.
N4 — The Cancellation Deficit (measurement)
The L1 Fourier mass exceeds the sign-blind proof threshold |ΣS| < D−S(0) by factors 204–297× at computable signatures, scaling as ≈0.75√S(0) = 2^(0.475B), with a statistically featureless (Gaussian-like) spectrum and no major-arc concentration. Consequence: absolute-value methods (circle method, decoupling, subgroup sums) are quantitatively excluded — a proof must capture phase-level cancellation. Information-theoretic reading: the conjecture is carried by a 0.05B-bit entropy gap; a sign-blind proof would need 0.475B bits of phase information — ten times the price of the truth.

5. The two walls, quantified

Wall 1 — cycles: phase-exact evaluation
N(0) = 0 requires evaluating ΣₜS(t) exactly; every absolute-value bound is 204–297× (growing as 2^(0.475B)) above threshold. All exact structure found (renewal, invariance, sum rules) reorganizes but does not evaluate the sum.
Wall 2 — divergence: individual orbits
Density zero at explicit exponential rate is proved; emptiness is an individual-orbit statement invisible to every density argument (including Tao 2019). One integer riding the slab forever cannot be excluded by counting.

Background shadow: Conway (1972) proved generalized Collatz maps undecidable. The measured featurelessness of the spectrum is consistent with — though of course not evidence for — the possibility that no proof exists in current axioms.

8. Program forward

The standing program: (i) external evaluation of N1–N4 (arXiv note / MathOverflow for Q1–Q3); (ii) machine formalization of the proved layer (Lean, via ccchallenge.org) so correctness is verifiable without trust; (iii) literature watch on the two walls — any movement on signed character sums or individual-orbit rigidity re-opens the attack with this map as the starting position. The problem is held, not abandoned.

8.1 First Wall-2 offensive executed (script 161)

The Applegate–Lagarias inverse-tree method was reimplemented and run: exact enumeration of the pruned inverse tree (30M nodes) yields unconditional density certificates climbing to γ = 0.796 at (E=6, depth 24) — faithfully approaching the published tree-search value 0.81 and confirming that raw enumeration saturates below the 2003 frontier of 0.84. The identified concrete project: implementing the Krasikov–Lagarias difference-inequality system at congruence levels beyond their mod 3⁹, where modern compute could plausibly move the published exponent — the one point on either wall where raw computation can still advance the field.

Prime-D scan (A ≤ 300): twelve signatures have D = 2B−3A prime — (2,4), (3,5), (4,7), (5,8), (13,21), (56,89), (61,97), (69,110), (73,116), (76,121), (148,235), (185,294) — empirical density ≈ 1/ln D, heuristically infinite. At every such signature the cycle problem is a single Gauss-period value (prime-D reduction, §2).
