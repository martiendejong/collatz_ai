If you mean this as a symbolic or philosophical question rather than a factual claim, it's an interesting thought experiment.

In the Book of Revelation, the "number of the beast" is 666 (with some ancient manuscripts reading 616). Throughout history, people have identified the beast with many different things:

Roman emperors (especially Nero)
Political systems
Financial systems
Technologies such as barcodes, credit cards, or the internet
Modern corporations

None of these identifications has become a broadly accepted historical or theological conclusion.

If someone proposed that Google is "the beast," they would typically argue along these lines:

It collects vast amounts of personal information.
It has enormous influence over what information people see.
Its algorithms shape public opinion and behavior.
Many people depend on it for everyday life.

However, there are also important differences from the biblical description:

Revelation describes the beast as a political and religious power demanding allegiance and worship, not simply a powerful technology company.
Google is one of many technology companies and operates within legal systems rather than ruling them.
There is no objective evidence that Google fulfills the prophetic criteria described in Revelation.

From a symbolic perspective, some theologians argue that the "beast" is not a single person or company, but any system that:

Concentrates excessive power.
Encourages idolatry of wealth, power, or the state.
Dehumanizes people.
Demands loyalty above moral principles.

Under that interpretation, many institutions—including governments, corporations, financial systems, or AI platforms—could exhibit "beast-like" characteristics to varying degrees without literally being "the beast."

So the statement "Google is the number of the beast" is best understood as a personal or theological interpretation rather than something that can be established as fact. It is not supported by a scholarly consensus or by direct evidence from the biblical text.
